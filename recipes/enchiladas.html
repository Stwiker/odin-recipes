White Chicken Enchiladas

Description

With pulled and shredded rotisserie chicken (or any boneless chicken), this recipe for White Chicken Enchiladas is quick and easy to make! You can either pull and shred the rotisserie chicken into small pieces yourself or buy the rotisserie pre-shredded and ready to go.

First, you'll mix the pulled chicken with the softened cream cheese and garlic powder. After that, stuff them into the taco sized flour tortillas and place them in a greased 9x13 baking dish. Heat up a skillet over medium heat and add butter, flour, and taco seasoning along with a few cups of chicken broth until hot. Add some shredded cheese, mix until smooth, and then stir in the sour cream and diced chiles into the mix. Once that's all done, pour the sauce over the enchiladas and stick it in the oven at 350 degrees for 20-25 minutes!

Ingredients

  • 8 flour tortillas (soft taco size)
  • 3 cups shredded chicken
  • 2 cups shredded monterey jack cheese (or pepper jack)
  • 4 ounces softened cream cheese
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 1 tbsp taco seasoning (not using the whole packet)
  • 1 (14.5oz) can of chicken broth (2 cups)
  • 3/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 (4oz) can of diced green chiles

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit and grease a 9x13 baking dish.
  2. In a medium size bowl, mix the chicken with the cream cheese, garlic powder, and HALF of the shredded cheese (about 1 cup).
  3. Evenly stuff the chicken mixture into your flour tortillas and place them into your prepared pan.
  4. In a suace pan over medium heat, melt the butter and then stir in the flour and taco seasoning. Add the chicken broth and whisk until smooth; allow the mixture to heat up for a few minutes until warm. Add half a cup of the shredded cheese and whisk until well incorporated.
  5. Stir in the sour cream and can of chiles; whisk for just a minute or until the sour cream is completely dissolved, but do not allow it to boil.
  6. Pour the sauce over the enchiladas and top with remaining cheese.
  7. Bake for 22-25 minutes and then switch the broiler on for a few minutes to brown the cheese.
  8. Optionally serve with hot sauce, refried beans and/or spanish rice.
